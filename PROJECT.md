# Depth Notes

**Strava for divers** — a logbook, a statistics app, and a world dive encyclopedia.

## What it is

Divers log every dive. Every piece of data — temperature, sightings, visibility, conditions — is attached to a real dive logged by a real person.

The personal side is a logbook with statistics. The collective side is an encyclopedia: pick a site, see what people saw, when, and under what conditions.

## Who it's for

All divers — recreational, technical, freediving, instructors. The data model accommodates the deepest case; the UI never forces casual divers to deal with it.

## What makes it different

Queryable global geo/time data. Other apps log dives. Depth Notes turns a million logbooks into a searchable atlas.

## Principles

1. **Privacy first.** Users own their data. Nothing leaves the device without opt-in. Anonymous use is always possible.
2. **Offline first.** A diver on a boat with no signal must be able to log a dive.
3. **Everything anchors to a dive.** No standalone posts, no decoupled photos. If you didn't log a dive, you didn't contribute.
4. **Start small, grow steadily.** Every feature ships end-to-end. Architecture stays clean as the app grows.

## Vision (directional)

- Cosmetic gamification: levels, badges, streaks, a personal aquarium of species seen.
- Trusted contributors curate the global catalog of sites and species.
- World dive encyclopedia: site → temperature ranges, common species, visibility, recent conditions.
- Curated, expandable species catalog. Users submit missing species for review.
- Dive computer import (post-1.0).
- Opt-in, anonymized environmental data shared with research institutions, only when the project is mature.

## What it's not

- Not a social network. No standalone posts, follows-as-feed, or comment threads.
- Not a dive planner.
- Not ad-supported.
- Not a place where personal data is sold.

## Tone

Strava-adjacent but quieter. Adventurous, community-driven, professional. Dark by default, deep ocean palette, generous negative space.

## Monetization

Free. If revenue is ever needed: donations first, then opt-in anonymized research-data partnerships. Never ads, never selling user-identifiable data.
